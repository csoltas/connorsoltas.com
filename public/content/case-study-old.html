Technical prototyping with data

In late 2022, I conceived and built a tool for Uber designers to work on cartography and geospatial visualizations in tandem.

That tool helped me lead a redesign of the Uber driver/courier app's most used feature -- the main map -- from the ground up.

The tooling I built sped up the redesign while improving the quality of every stage of output along the way.

Why I made this

I was tasked with redesigning the app's main map, which had become a jumbled mess drivers found useless.

A tiny web app making it easy for me (and future designers) to prototype new basemap styles & data visualizations interactively, at full fidelity, with real Uber data.

The new design completely reshaped our main map into a stark canvas for data visualization.

The tooling I built sped up that redesign, while improving the quality of every intermediate stage of output.

The concept

While I wasn't tasked with building a new tool, I knew it could help me redesign the map better and faster.

So I set out to build the simplest sufficient tool: a tiny web app to prototype map visuals & interaction design, at full fidelity, with real Uber data.

Here's what I did

  1. Learned enough Mapbox GL JS and DeckGL [and a few other libraries] to do the job.
  2. Built the web app I needed. Documented a workflow that I taught to others.
  3. Used the web app to redesign the map from first principles.
  4. Iterated on map design in constant communication with non-map designers and Uber's product execs.

Impact discussion

My team was only able to confidently propose such a radical new direction because we could easily explore all its downstream consequences.

And Uber execs could see those consequences, too -- saving them time that often gets lost in slide decks, and enabling conversations that were just too abstract to have beforehand.

This sped up the redesign project while also improving the quality of every intermediate stage of output.

Even further, the tool I built acted as a prototype we could bring directly into user testing, letting us gather feedback that only built confidence in our direction.

As a result, the redesign encouraged Uber to launch a unified visualization of potential earnings on the main map. [See other case study.]
